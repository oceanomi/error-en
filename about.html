Born in 1973 in Patagonia, Argentina.

She studied journalism in Buenos Aires and began her career in the former Yugoslavia, specializing in the Balkans.

She covered the war in Iraq and from there kept a blog (Eye, view from Iraq) that became the first Spanish-language blog to be published as a book.

In Istanbul, she covered Turkey and the Middle East.

There she made a documentary (The Iraqi Workout) about the only gym for women in the north of Iraq (acquired by YL2, Finnish state TV).

She worked as editor of blogs for the Argentine newspaper Clarin.

She moved to the humanitarian field as public information officer for UNHCR (United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees) in the south of Latin America.

In recent years, she has been writing film scripts and continuing with humanitarian work but from a coaching perspective. In the meantime, she keeps a woven record of her own mistakes, which have become the work presented here.

She lives in the south of France, where she has participated in several group exhibitions.
